## How to Read Dot Plots Like a Pro

Dot plots are deceptively simple-looking charts that pack a lot of information. Here's how to extract maximum insight from them.

## Step 1: Understand the Axes

The **horizontal axis** (x-axis) represents the values in your dataset. The **vertical stacking** of dots represents how many times each value appears (frequency).

## Step 2: Look for Central Tendency

Where are most of the dots concentrated? This cluster represents the **center** of your data — roughly where the mean or median would be.

## Step 3: Assess the Spread

How spread out are the dots? A **narrow cluster** means low variability; **widely scattered dots** indicate high variability in your data.

## Step 4: Identify Key Patterns

### Clusters
Groups of dots bunched together indicate **common values** in your dataset.

### Gaps
Empty spaces between dots suggest values that **don't appear** in your data. These might indicate natural groupings.

### Outliers
Isolated dots far from the main group are **outliers** — unusual values that may warrant further investigation.

### Shape
- **Symmetric**: Data is evenly distributed around the center
- **Skewed right**: Most data is on the left with a tail extending right
- **Skewed left**: Most data is on the right with a tail extending left
- **Bimodal**: Two distinct clusters suggesting two groups in your data
