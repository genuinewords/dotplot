## Why Real-World Data Matters

Textbook examples use neat, perfectly distributed numbers. Real data is messy — it has outliers, skewness, clusters, and gaps. Dot plots shine with real data because they show **every individual point**, letting you see patterns that summary statistics hide.

## Salary Data: Spotting Income Inequality

A classic use for dot plots is visualizing salary distributions. When you plot individual salaries:

- **Right-skewed distributions** are immediately visible — a few high earners pull the mean above the median
- **Clusters** reveal common pay bands (e.g., most employees at $55-75K)
- **Outliers** stand out clearly — executives earning 3-5× the median
- **The gap between mean and median** becomes intuitive when you see the actual dots

This is why economists and HR analysts use dot plots to communicate pay equity findings to non-technical audiences.

## Exam Score Data: Understanding Student Performance

In education, dot plots of test scores reveal:

- **Bimodal distributions** — two groups of students performing at different levels
- **Grade boundaries** — clusters just above/below passing thresholds
- **Effectiveness of interventions** — compare pre- and post-test distributions
- **Individual outliers** — students who need extra support or advanced challenges

Teachers can glance at a dot plot and immediately know whether a class is performing consistently or whether subgroups need different approaches.

## Sports Statistics: Comparing Player Performance

Sports analysts use dot plots to:

- **Visualize scoring distributions** — most players cluster around league average, with a few stars far above
- **Compare positions** — overlay dot plots for guards vs. forwards vs. centers
- **Track trends** — plot a player's game-by-game performance across a season
- **Identify breakouts** — a dot far from the cluster signals an unusually good (or bad) game

The shape of a sports dot plot often follows a log-normal pattern — many average performers and a thin tail of superstars.

## Key Takeaways

1. **Dot plots preserve individual data points** — unlike histograms, no information is lost
2. **Skewness is visible at a glance** — you don't need to calculate a skewness coefficient
3. **Outliers can't hide** — every data point gets its own dot
4. **Clusters reveal subgroups** — multimodal patterns jump out immediately
5. **They work for any domain** — salaries, scores, sports, science, business metrics
